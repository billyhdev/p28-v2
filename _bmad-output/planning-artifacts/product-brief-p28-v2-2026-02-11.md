# Product Brief: p28-v2

## Executive Summary

p28-v2 is an all-in-one online platform that removes physical barriers to church involvement, promotes genuine belonging, and connects members and ministries globally. It serves existing Christians, people curious about faith, and ministries that want to reach and care for people at scale. Today’s tools are mostly hybrid and in-person-first—they extend attendance online but don’t foster real relationships or make the internet the primary place for church life. p28-v2 is designed as the primary digital home: a clean, engaging platform for both members and ministers that gets connection right first. The aim is for users to see it as "a modern Christian’s essential tool"—enabled by early use of AI, depth of ministry workflows, trust from churches, and a deliberate balance of church purpose and social engagement, with a global and multi-language mindset. The timing is right because technology is pervasive yet many churches are still behind in using it to reach and nurture their people.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Churches and seekers face a mismatch: physical location is still treated as the main way to belong, while many people are online-first, globally scattered, or unable to attend in person. Existing Christians, people curious about church, and ministries that want global reach lack a platform built for online as the primary experience. Current options are hybrid and physical-first—they add streaming or basic digital touchpoints but don’t organize community well, don’t put online ministry first, and don’t create the kind of belonging that makes people feel invested. The result is a gap: no seamless, engaging app that people are excited to open and that truly invokes belonging and connection across distance and language.

### Problem Impact

If this gap persists, potential Christians may never be reached, ministries stay local and don’t scale, and people drift when there’s no strong digital anchor. Smaller ministries feel the pain most (limited staff and resources), along with curious people who have no low-barrier way to explore faith. Churches keep using the internet as a secondary channel instead of as the main place where community and discipleship happen.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Today’s solutions are mostly hybrid and rooted in physical attendance and membership. At best they let people attend services online; they rarely go beyond that. They are in-person-first and don’t foster genuine relationships built and sustained online. There is no easy, seamless app that people are excited to open like the social apps they use daily. Building a product that is full-featured and also creates real excitement, engagement, and belonging is hard—and most incumbents are not designed or incentivized to make the internet the primary way to reach and foster members.

### Proposed Solution

p28-v2 is an all-in-one online solution for ministries that want to reach globally or better engage members who are more technologically inclined. The first priority is getting connection right: users connected to their ministries in a way that is engaging and meaningful. The product is a clean, engaging, and enabling platform for both members and ministers—not a side channel, but the main place for community and ministry. Success looks like users saying, "This is a modern Christian’s essential tool."

### Key Differentiators

- **Early adoption of AI** to ship capabilities that aren’t widely on the market yet and to position the product in a way that actively engages users.
- **Hard to copy:** Depth of ministry workflows, trust from churches, a careful balance of "church" and "social," and a global, multi-language approach.
- **Uniquely us:** A single, coherent platform for members, ministers, and churches as a whole—one place for belonging, communication, and ministry.
- **Why now:** Technology is everywhere, yet many churches have not fully adapted to use it to reach and foster their members; they remain behind and stuck in old patterns. That creates a clear opportunity to lead with an online-first, connection-first platform.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**Members (e.g. Billy)**  
Billy is a member of a church in Surrey, BC, involved in multiple ministries. He wants one place to see upcoming events and connect with others in those ministries; his church and ministries have members in different countries. Today he juggles the Bible app for devotionals, Zoom for services, and Discord to stay connected—no single home for church life. Success for Billy is: one app where he can get everything he needs for church and church life.

**Ministries (e.g. Annie)**  
A ministry is an organization within a church (e.g. Young Adult, Youth). Annie is a ministry lead at All Nations Church (ANC) in Surrey, BC, and oversees young adult and youth groups. She wants to facilitate discussion and foster members. She currently uses Discord, Zoom, and the Bible app to cultivate church life and also needs administrative and collaborative tools with other church leaders. Success for Annie is: "I can't live without this tool as a church leader."

### Secondary Users

**Ministers**  
Ordained leaders who primarily focus on delivering messages and are involved in high-level operations and the direction of the church.

**Admins**  
Church leaders who are not ordained; they may also deliver messages and are responsible for day-to-day operations and planning.

**Curious seekers / guests**  
People exploring faith or a church with a low barrier to entry: they can join as a guest, control what they see and which notifications they get, and explore groups and ministries that resonate with them.

### User Journey

**Member (Billy):**

- **Discovery:** Billy finds the app either by joining a church that already uses it as their platform or by searching for it in the app store.
- **Onboarding:** He joins an organization/church/ministry as a guest, chooses which notifications he wants, and can explore other groups and ministries that "speak" to him.
- **Core usage:** He uses the app to stay connected to the organization he's joined, receive notifications for upcoming events and services he can join, and connect with other members.
- **Success moment:** He feels: "Without this app I would never have learned about Christianity and would have never been this involved in my faith life."
- **Long-term:** The app becomes part of his daily routine alongside his other social apps.

## Success Metrics

**User success**

- **Outcomes:** Users stay in the loop with what's happening at church, are engaged in conversations, and are encouraged to participate.
- **"It's working" signal:** Users no longer need multiple apps for church life; this app becomes the single place to stay connected to their church and fellow members.
- **Behaviors we measure:** Time in app per week, check-ins (e.g. to events/services/groups), and engagement with content (e.g. announcements, events, messages).
- **Near-term (e.g. 3 months):** A product we can showcase to members and leaders that they're genuinely excited to use and that demonstrates core value.

### Business Objectives

- **3 months:** Product showcase-ready; members and leaders are excited to use it; early proof that one app can replace multiple tools for church life.
- **12 months:** Support ministries from across the world and connect them to each other; the product is on the path to being an essential tool for church members.
- **We optimize for:** Engagement and church/ministry adoption (not just downloads).
- **"This is working":** Leaders and members say they can't live without it (qualitative signal we can track via feedback and retention).

### Key Performance Indicators

- **Engagement:** Time in app per week (e.g. average or median); check-ins per user per week/month; engagement with content (views, RSVPs, replies).
- **Adoption:** Number of churches/ministries onboarded; number of active members; retention (e.g. % still active after 30/90 days).
- **Leading indicators:** Messages sent/received, group/conversation participation, event attendance (in-app or linked), and qualitative feedback ("can't live without it," "don't use other apps for church anymore").

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

- Multiple churches/ministries/organizations can be onboarded.
- Users can join these groups and stay connected to the groups they join.
- Users can connect with each other (messaging/connection within groups).
- Leaders can broadcast events and announcements to users and reach the right people.
- **Ship order:** Connection and ministry first; then add features that leaders and administrators value.

### Out of Scope for MVP

- **Nice to have, not in v1:** Multi-lingual communication (e.g. Korean and English users communicating with each other); admin dashboard and status view of their groups/ministry.
- **Explicitly no for now:** Building our own streaming or video service; payments/tithing.
- **Deferred to v2+:** Advanced roles and permissions; minister-specific features (e.g. share verses, messages, encouragement to specific groups; tools to foster members).

### MVP Success Criteria

- Our church network uses the app as their **main app for church life and administration** (primary channel, not a side tool).
- Decision point for scaling beyond MVP: **when the features we have are not enough** (user/leader demand and usage drive the roadmap).

### Future Vision

- **2–3 years:** The product is an **essential tool for church life** with a **full admin/leader toolkit** to foster and administer within groups (plus multi-language, minister features, and advanced roles as we add them).
